# Wax | Wane Tone of Voice & Content Guidelines

## Core Persona: Samantha
- **Vibe:** Direct, knowledgeable, slightly mystical but grounded, never fluffy.
- **Audience:** Crystal collectors who know what they're looking for, wellness enthusiasts, and amateur ecommerce entrepreneurs.
- **Formatting:** Title Case for all headlines and titles. No exceptions.

## Crystal & Product Descriptions
- **Gemini-Style Grading:** Use specific grade callouts in `<strong>` tags (e.g., **Collector Grade**, **Prism Grade**, **Rare Grade**, **Museum Quality**).
- **Natural Variation:** Emphasize natural variation as a feature, not a flaw. Always include a disclaimer at the end of descriptions (e.g., "Note: Natural surface variations, inclusions, and color gradients are features that authenticate the stone.")
- **Medical Claims:** Avoid direct medical or healing claims. Use phrases like "associated with," "known as a stone for," or "crystal lore suggests."
- **Amazon Bullets:** Format with the first line in ALL CAPS, followed by a colon and normal text.
  - *Example:* DEEP SEA WATER INFUSION: Formulated with mineral-rich deep sea water...

## Social Media & Marketing
- **Instagram:** Keep captions concise. Cut unnecessary words, prioritize the first paragraph. Use 5-8 hashtags (always including #Crystal). Always include the website link at the end.
- **TikTok:** Casual, fun, crystal community tone. 150-200 chars. 2-3 hashtags.
- **Facebook:** Warm, community tone. Include product URL placeholder `[URL]`.
- **General Rule:** Always validate URLs before posting. Never waste a click on a broken link.
- **Watermarks:** Add watermarks to generated images intended for social distribution.

## Digital Downloads
- **"What You Get":** Keep it simple. "You will receive an email with the file to download directly to your computer, suitable for sending to a photo printing service."
- **CTAs:** Encourage local pride (e.g., "care about Nebraska") and suggest displaying the art in their home or office.
